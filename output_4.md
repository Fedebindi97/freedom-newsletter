This edition of the Nika newsletter covers global developments impacting freedom and human rights from **June 22 - June 29, 2026**. We examine events through the lens of Timothy Snyder's five pillars of freedom: Sovereignty, Unpredictability, Mobility, Factuality, and Solidarity, alongside instances of extreme power imbalance.

***

### FACTUALITY & UNPREDICTABILITY: Press Freedom Under Siege

The past week has seen concerning developments regarding press freedom and access to information, impacting the ability of individuals to form a firm, shared understanding of truth and make free choices.

*   **Turkey:** Ahead of the NATO Summit in Ankara (July 7-8), Turkish authorities have been criticized for their crackdown on journalists and media freedom. Journalist Yıldız Tar was detained on June 23 and arrested on June 25. Evrensel reporter Doğa Baskan was also arrested this week in connection with a news report. Furthermore, accreditation requests from numerous independent media organizations, including İlke TV, Cumhuriyet, Medyascope, and NOW TV, to cover the summit were rejected without explanation. In a move affecting digital freedom, access to several LGBTQ+ dating apps and social networking platforms was blocked by Istanbul courts in early June.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Media and Law Studies Association (MLSA)](https://www.medyalaw.org/en/) that are working to defend journalists and freedom of expression in Turkey.
*   **United States:** The U.S. Justice Department recently withdrew subpoenas that sought to compel reporters from The Washington Post and The Wall Street Journal to testify before a grand jury. This decision followed strong criticism from press freedom advocates who argued that such actions, alongside earlier FBI searches of a Washington Post journalist's home in January, threatened constitutionally guaranteed press freedom.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Committee to Protect Journalists](https://cpj.org/) or the [ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/) (for First Amendment rights) that advocate for press freedom.

### MOBILITY: Challenges to Migrant and Refugee Autonomy

The ability to move physically and socially in autonomy, a core aspect of mobility, faced significant setbacks this past week for migrants and asylum seekers.

*   **United States:** The U.S. Supreme Court delivered a 6-3 decision on June 25, allowing the Trump administration to terminate Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for approximately 350,000 Haitian and 6,100 Syrian immigrants. This ruling means that many individuals, some of whom have lived and worked in the U.S. for years, could now face deportation to countries that the State Department itself warns against traveling to due to widespread violence and instability. The UN Human Rights Chief, Volker Türk, has broadly criticized the dehumanization of migrants and refugees, including in the US, in the context of global migration policies.
    *   *Call to Action:* Consider supporting organizations like the [American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA)](https://www.aila.org/) or [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/united-states) that advocate for migrant rights and accountability in the U.S.
*   **European Union:** The EU's new Return Regulation, which allows for stricter migration measures and the creation of "offshore deportation centers," was approved by the European Parliament on June 17, 2026. While the legislation awaits final formal approval from member states, critics argue it risks expanding detention, weakening safeguards, and has little regard for people's safety, dignity, and rights. Discussions about dangerous EU momentum towards "offshore deportation centers" in Africa and Central Asia are ongoing.
    *   *Call to Action:* Engage with organizations like [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/europe/central-asia) or local migrant support groups in Europe that are monitoring the implementation of the EU Pact.

### SOLIDARITY & SOVEREIGNTY: Collective Action and Rights Affirmation

The collective effort to build empowering structures and the factual ability to change the world according to one's values were evident in both challenges and victories.

*   **Hungary:** A significant victory for LGBTQ+ rights was observed with the peaceful execution of the Budapest Pride march on June 27, approved by police. This follows a landmark ruling by the Court of Justice of the European Union on April 21, 2026, which found that Hungary's 2021 "child protection" law, restricting content related to sexual orientation and gender identity, violated EU fundamental rights. While this signals a positive shift under the new government led by Prime Minister Péter Magyar, who took office earlier in June, civil society groups emphasize that other anti-LGBTQI+ laws remain in force and are calling for their repeal and full legal equality, including same-sex marriage and legal gender recognition.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support Hungarian LGBTQ+ rights organizations like [Budapest Pride](https://budapestpride.hu/en/) or [Háttér Society](https://hatter.hu/en) in their ongoing advocacy for legal and social change.
*   **Western Sahara:** The National Council reaffirmed its full solidarity with Sahrawi civilian prisoners held in Moroccan prisons, urging the United Nations, international bodies, and human rights organizations to demand their immediate and unconditional release.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support groups advocating for the rights of the Sahrawi people and political prisoners, such as those working with the [Sahara Press Service](http://www.spsrasd.info/news/).

### POWER IMBALANCE: Erosion of Fundamental Rights and Accountability

Instances of extreme power imbalance continue to pose significant threats to human dignity and freedom worldwide.

*   **Hong Kong:** As the sixth anniversary of the National Security Law approaches (June 30, 2020), concerns persist regarding Beijing's tightening social control and erosion of fundamental freedoms in Hong Kong.
*   **Tunisia:** Rights defender Sihem Bensedrine was handed a 25-year sentence on June 26, highlighting a severe curtailment of freedom of expression and justice.
*   **Ukraine:** The UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine reported a concerning increase in civilian casualties from short-range drones in May 2026, marking the highest number since February 2022. Overall, May 2026 saw the highest total number of civilians killed and injured (274 killed, 1,763 injured) since April 2022.

At its core, freedom demands shared truth and the space to form our own convictions. When information is stifled and movement constrained, our capacity for genuine choice and an authentic understanding of the world diminishes. Yet, true freedom is never a solitary endeavor; it blossoms through collective action, asserting our values and building structures that empower all. The ongoing struggle is against forces that deny this agency, distort our truths, and restrict our movement, eroding our ability to shape our own destinies.
