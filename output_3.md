*   The will to shape one's own world, whether against an invading army or an unresponsive government, remains the core of the struggle for freedom. We see this in Ukraine's determined resistance to being erased and in the collective political awakening of Kenya's youth, who are organizing to claim their future rather than have it decided for them.

*   Freedom is not only lost on the battlefield but is also eroded by the stroke of a pen. In the United Kingdom, vague laws risk silencing the unpredictable thoughts that challenge power, while in Nigeria, an economic policy without adequate social support has crippled the ability of millions to simply move, eat, and live with autonomy.

*   The desperate flight of civilians in Sudan is a stark reminder that freedom begins with the most fundamental security: the ability to live without constant fear. Where the social fabric has disintegrated, all other liberties become impossible, underscoring that our freedom is never truly individual, but is built upon a collective promise of safety and support.
