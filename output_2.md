## SECURITY AND CONFLICT
*   In Ukraine, while the overall frequency of large-scale Russian strikes appeared to lessen in June 2026, Ukrainian forces have continued to conduct long-range strikes against Russian oil infrastructure in response.
*   The humanitarian crisis in Sudan is deepening. The UN warns that escalating violence is pushing millions to the brink of famine and creating an environment where civilians are constantly forced to flee for their safety.

## DEMOCRACY AND VOTING
*   Youth in Kenya are leading a significant push for large-scale voter registration ahead of the 2027 general elections, where they are expected to be the largest voting bloc. This movement has emerged from recent youth-led protests against corruption and the rising cost of living. Organizers are using social media to mobilize and assist young people in registering to vote, signaling a growing political engagement among a new generation aiming to influence the country's future.

## FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION
*   The United Kingdom's Online Safety Act, which became law in 2023, is drawing continuing criticism from digital rights groups for its potential to curtail freedom of expression. The law requires online platforms to proactively remove illegal or "harmful" content, with executives facing fines or prison time for non-compliance. Critics argue that the broad and vague definitions of "harmful" content could lead to the censorship of legitimate political speech and will have a chilling effect on what people feel they can say online.

## ECONOMIC POWER
*   In Nigeria, the removal of a long-standing fuel subsidy in May 2023 has led to an ongoing severe cost-of-living crisis, disproportionately affecting the poor. The decision caused petrol prices to skyrocket, triggering rampant inflation that has seen the cost of food and transportation surge. While the government insists the reform was necessary to stabilize public finances, millions of Nigerians continue to be pushed into acute food insecurity, and critics argue that promised social protections have been inadequate to cushion the blow.
