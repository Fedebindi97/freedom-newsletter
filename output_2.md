### FACTUALITY & UNPREDICTABILITY: Press Freedom Under Siege

The past week has seen concerning developments regarding press freedom and access to information, impacting the ability of individuals to form a firm, shared understanding of truth and make free choices.

*   **Turkey:** Ahead of the NATO Summit scheduled for July 7-8 in Ankara, Turkish authorities have been criticized for their crackdown on journalists and media freedom. Journalist Yıldız Tar was detained on June 23 and subsequently arrested on June 25. Evrensel reporter Doğa Baskan was also arrested during the same week in connection with a news report. Furthermore, accreditation requests from numerous independent media organizations, such as İlke TV, Cumhuriyet, Medyascope, and NOW TV, to cover the summit were rejected without explanation. In a move affecting digital freedom, access to several LGBTQ+ dating apps and social networking platforms, including Hornet, Jack'd, HeeSay, Taimi, SCRUFF, Surge, ROMEO, BEARWW, Shuggr, and Daddyhunt, was blocked in Turkey by decisions from Istanbul courts in early June.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Media and Law Studies Association (MLSA)](https://www.medyalaw.org/en/) that are working to defend journalists and freedom of expression in Turkey.
*   **United States:** The U.S. Justice Department recently withdrew subpoenas that sought to compel reporters from The Washington Post and The Wall Street Journal to testify before a grand jury. This came after strong criticism from press freedom advocates who argued that such actions, along with earlier FBI searches of a Washington Post journalist's home in January, threatened constitutionally guaranteed press freedom.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Committee to Protect Journalists](https://cpj.org/) or the [ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/) (for First Amendment rights) that advocate for press freedom.

### MOBILITY: Challenges to Migrant and Refugee Autonomy

The ability to move physically and socially in autonomy, a core aspect of mobility, faced significant setbacks this past week for migrants and asylum seekers.

*   **United States:** The U.S. Supreme Court delivered a 6-3 decision on June 25, allowing the Trump administration to terminate Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for approximately 350,000 Haitian and 6,100 Syrian immigrants. This ruling means that many of whom have lived and worked in the U.S. for years, could now face deportation to countries that the State Department itself warns against traveling to due to widespread violence and instability. The UN Human Rights Chief, Volker Türk, has broadly criticized the dehumanization of migrants and refugees, including in the US, in the context of global migration policies.
    *   *Call to Action:* Consider supporting organizations like the [American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA)](https://www.aila.org/) or [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/united-states) (which has reported on ICE deaths) that are advocating for migrant rights and accountability in the U.S.
*   **European Union:** The EU's new Return Regulation, which allows for stricter migration measures and the creation of "offshore deportation centers," was approved by the European Parliament on June 17, 2026. While the legislation awaits final formal approval from member states, critics argue it risks expanding detention, weakening safeguards, and has little regard for people's safety, dignity, and rights. Discussions about dangerous EU momentum towards "offshore deportation centers" in Africa and Central Asia are ongoing.
    *   *Call to Action:* Engage with organizations like [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/europe/central-asia) or local migrant support groups in Europe that are monitoring the implementation of the EU Pact.
