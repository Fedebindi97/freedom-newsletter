## SECURITY AND CONFLICT
*   Civilian casualties in Ukraine have sharply risen following one of the most intense Russian attacks on Kyiv this week. An 11-hour drone and missile assault on the capital on Thursday, July 2, killed at least 25 people and injured over 90, with residential buildings and other civilian infrastructure being hit. [The UN has reported a 40% surge in civilian casualties from Russian air attacks in the last 18 months](https://www.united24.media/news/un-reports-40-surge-in-ukrainian-civilian-casualties-from-russian-air-attacks).
*   In the Middle East, an Israeli drone strike on Saturday, June 27, killed two Palestinian siblings, 15-year-old Islam Moussa and 30-year-old Abdullah Moussa, in a camp for displaced people in southern Gaza, despite a supposed ceasefire. Meanwhile, in the occupied West Bank, a coalition of Israeli settler groups is reportedly pushing forward with a plan to establish [100 new outposts in areas theoretically under Palestinian Authority control](https://truthout.org/articles/israeli-settlers-are-pushing-a-plan-to-establish-100-new-outposts-in-west-bank/), a move that would further fragment Palestinian territory.
*   The UN is warning of a "human rights catastrophe" unfolding in El Obeid, the capital of Sudan's North Kordofan state, which has been under siege-like conditions for 18 months. In the last few weeks of June alone, relentless drone strikes by both the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) have [killed at least 45 civilians and targeted critical infrastructure like markets, schools, and fuel stations](https://www.unannews.com/sudan-s-people-subject-to-relentless-drone-strikes-in-el-obeid/).

## DEMOCRACY AND VOTING
*   In Tunisia, the government of President Kais Saied continues to dismantle democratic institutions and suppress dissent. Civil society organizations are increasingly being criminalized, with activists, journalists, and lawyers facing imprisonment. [Recent actions include the targeting of anti-racism campaigners and the closure of organizations focused on migrant and women's rights](https://globalissues.org/news/2026/07/01/32822).
*   Belarus's authoritarian leader Alexander Lukashenko has pardoned 28 political prisoners in a decree marking the country's Independence Day. While a welcome development for those released, human rights groups emphasize that hundreds of political prisoners remain incarcerated, and the move is seen as part of an effort to improve relations with the West [rather than a fundamental shift in the regime's repressive policies](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/07/01/belarus-authoritarian-leader-pardons-28-political-prisoners-effort-ease-ties-with-west/0761e564-37a5-11ef-8418-24d64215169a_story.html).
*   As voting rights face challenges across the United States, New Jersey has taken a step to expand access to the ballot by enacting the "John R. Lewis Voter Empowerment Act." The new law strengthens protections against voter suppression and intimidation, and [improves access to voting materials for citizens with limited English proficiency](https://www.wrnjradio.com/2026/07/04/new-jersey-enacts-john-r-lewis-voter-empowerment-act/).

## FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION
*   In the lead-up to the NATO summit, scheduled for July 7-8 in Ankara, Turkish authorities have intensified their crackdown on civil liberties. A 13-day ban on all public demonstrations has been imposed in the capital, and in recent days, [police have arrested hundreds of people, including political activists, lawyers, and journalists](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/06/25/turkiye-crackdown-ahead-nato-summit).
*   Iranian political journalist Omid Faraghat was arrested on June 29th after being summoned by security police. He faces charges of 'disturbing public opinion' and 'acting against national security'. [This is the third time Faraghat has been prosecuted for his critical reporting, and he is now the seventh journalist imprisoned in Iran](https://www.ifj.org/media-centre/news/detail/category/press-releases/article/iran-political-journalist-arrested).
*   China has launched a new three-month internet censorship campaign called "Qinglang," aimed at rectifying "chaos in account name information." While officially targeting issues like fraud and misinformation, the campaign serves to tighten the Chinese Communist Party's control over online discourse and [further suppress dissenting voices and cultural expression, with a significant impact on Tibetans](https://www.savetibet.org/china-launches-three-month-crackdown-on-the-internet/).

## ECONOMIC POWER
*   A recent report has found that 88 of the largest and most profitable corporations in the United States paid no federal income tax in 2025. These same companies collectively spent nearly $852 million on lobbying and campaign contributions over the last three election cycles, [creating what the report calls a "self-reinforcing loop" where corporate cash influences policy to secure further tax breaks](https://www.citizen.org/news/corporate-tax-dodgers-lobbying-and-political-spending-records-reveal-self-reinforcing-loop/).
*   A Swedish court has ordered Google to pay nearly $2 billion to a price-comparison service, Pricerunner, for abusing its market dominance. The court found that Google favored its own comparison shopping service in its search results, harming competitors and consumers. [The ruling is one of the largest in a Swedish competition case and marks a significant decision against the power of big tech](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-07-01/google-hit-with-2-billion-antitrust-judgment-for-skewing-shopping-searches).
*   Leading artificial intelligence companies are significantly increasing their lobbying efforts to shape regulation in their favor. OpenAI, for example, is pursuing a "reverse federalism" strategy, lobbying individual states to pass industry-friendly laws to create a de facto national standard. [This approach highlights the growing influence of AI companies on the political process and their attempts to preempt stronger federal oversight](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/05/20/openai-political-strategy-00158933).
