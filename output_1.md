The Nika newsletter focuses on global developments impacting freedom and human rights. This edition covers the period of June 22 - June 29, 2026, examining events through the lens of Timothy Snyder's five pillars of freedom: Sovereignty, Unpredictability, Mobility, Factuality, and Solidarity, as well as instances of extreme power imbalance.

***

### FACTUALITY & UNPREDICTABILITY: Press Freedom Under Siege

The past week has seen concerning developments regarding press freedom and access to information, impacting the ability of individuals to form a firm, shared understanding of truth and make free choices.

*   **Turkey:** Ahead of the NATO Summit, Turkish authorities have been criticized for their crackdown on journalists and media freedom. Journalist Yıldız Tar was detained, and two other journalists, including Evrensel reporter Doğa Baskan, were arrested. Furthermore, accreditation requests from numerous independent media organizations, such as İlke TV, Cumhuriyet, Medyascope, and NOW TV, to cover the summit were rejected without explanation. In a move affecting digital freedom, access to several LGBTQ+ dating apps and social networking platforms was blocked in Turkey by a decision from the Istanbul 5th Criminal Judgeship of Peace.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Media and Law Studies Association (MLSA)](https://www.medyalaw.org/) (based on search result, a link to their English site would be ideal if found) that are working to defend journalists and freedom of expression in Turkey.
*   **United States:** The U.S. Justice Department recently withdrew subpoenas that sought to compel reporters from The Washington Post and The Wall Street Journal to testify before a grand jury. This came after strong criticism from press freedom advocates who argued that such actions, along with earlier FBI searches of journalists' homes, threatened constitutionally guaranteed press freedom. Additionally, Public Knowledge filed comments with the Federal Communications Commission (FCC), warning that the FCC's actions against "The View" could threaten press freedom, arguing that government officials should not threaten regulated speakers with legal consequences for their editorial choices.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support organizations like the [Committee to Protect Journalists](https://cpj.org/) or the [ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/) (for First Amendment rights) that advocate for press freedom.
*   **Global Trend:** A new report, "The Atlas of Impunity," launched by Eurasia Group, identifies the erosion of press freedom as a central driver in the widening gap between accountable and impunity-prone countries.

### MOBILITY: Challenges to Migrant and Refugee Autonomy

The ability to move physically and socially in autonomy, a core aspect of mobility, faced significant setbacks this past week for migrants and asylum seekers.

*   **United States:** The U.S. Supreme Court delivered a decision allowing the Trump administration to terminate Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for hundreds of thousands of Haitian and Syrian immigrants. This ruling means that approximately 350,000 Haitians and 6,100 Syrians, many of whom have lived and worked in the U.S. for years, could now face deportation to countries that the State Department itself warns against traveling to due to violence and instability. Separately, the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, Volker Türk, called for independent oversight of the U.S. immigration detention system and investigations into a surge of deaths in Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) custody. Türk noted "inhuman conditions," inadequate healthcare, and the alarming use of solitary confinement in ICE facilities.
    *   *Call to Action:* Consider supporting organizations like the [American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA)](https://www.aila.org/) or [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/united-states) (which has reported on ICE deaths) that are advocating for migrant rights and accountability in the U.S.
*   **European Union:** The EU's new Pact on Migration and Asylum, which came into force on June 12, is raising concerns among disability rights groups for potentially weakening protections for migrants and asylum seekers with disabilities. Critics argue that the Pact lacks clear safeguards for disability-inclusive screening and reception procedures, risking wrongful detention and denial of necessary support. There are also ongoing discussions about dangerous EU momentum towards "offshore deportation centers" in Africa and Central Asia.
    *   *Call to Action:* Engage with organizations like [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/europe/central-asia) or local migrant support groups in Europe that are monitoring the implementation of the EU Pact.

### SOLIDARITY & SOVEREIGNTY: Collective Action and Rights Affirmation

The collective effort to build empowering structures and the factual ability to change the world according to one's values were evident in both challenges and victories.

*   **Hungary:** A significant victory for LGBTQ+ rights was observed with the peaceful execution of the Budapest Pride march on June 27, approved by police. This follows a landmark ruling by the Court of Justice of the European Union in April 2026, which found that Hungary's 2021 "child protection" law, restricting content related to sexual orientation and gender identity, violated EU fundamental rights. While this signals a positive shift under the new government, civil society groups emphasize that other anti-LGBTQI+ laws remain in force and are calling for their repeal and full legal equality, including same-sex marriage and legal gender recognition.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support Hungarian LGBTQ+ rights organizations like [Budapest Pride](https://budapestpride.hu/en/) or [Háttér Society](https://hatter.hu/en) (cited in search results as an LGBTQ rights group) in their ongoing advocacy for legal and social change.
*   **Western Sahara:** The National Council reaffirmed its full solidarity with Sahrawi civilian prisoners held in Moroccan prisons, urging the United Nations, international bodies, and human rights organizations to demand their immediate and unconditional release. The statement highlighted years of harsh detention conditions and retaliatory measures against the prisoners for their peaceful struggle.
    *   *Call to Action:* Support groups advocating for the rights of the Sahrawi people and political prisoners, such as those working with the [Sahara Press Service](http://www.spsrasd.info/news/) (where the news originated).
*   **Global Human Rights:** Amnesty International highlighted "31 wins for human rights" over the past six months, demonstrating the impact of collective action. These achievements include progress on a Convention on Crimes against Humanity and expanded access to abortion care in Europe.
*   **Right to Protest:** Globally, there are growing concerns about "regressive legislation and strong penalties for attending banned protests," signaling a tightening civic space.

### POWER IMBALANCE: Erosion of Fundamental Rights and Accountability

Instances of extreme power imbalance continue to pose significant threats to human dignity and freedom worldwide.

*   **Hong Kong:** Beijing is reportedly tightening social control six years after the imposition of the National Security Law, leading to ongoing concerns about accountability and fundamental freedoms.
*   **Mali:** Grave abuses are being committed amid renewed fighting, with reports of violations by the army, allied militias, and Islamist armed groups, often with impunity.
*   **Tunisia:** A rights defender was handed a 25-year sentence, highlighting a severe curtailment of freedom of expression and justice.
*   **Ukraine:** The UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine reported a concerning increase in civilian casualties from short-range drones in May 2026, marking the highest number since February 2022.
*   **U.S. Foreign Policy and Global Impunity:** The "Atlas of Impunity" report indicates that the U.S. under the current administration has declined in its ranking due to eroding public administration and rising inequality, impacting global networks upholding human rights. The U.S. has also withdrawn its participation in the Human Rights Council's Universal Periodic Review.
