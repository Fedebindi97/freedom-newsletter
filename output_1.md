## SECURITY AND CONFLICT
* A massive Russian missile and drone barrage [struck Kyiv and the surrounding regions](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jul/02/russia-launches-massive-drone-and-missile-attack-on-kyiv), killing dozens of civilians and destroying apartment buildings, as the unjust war continues to strip innocent people of their basic right to life and self-determination.
* Marking more than 1,000 days of devastating conflict, the Israeli military has intensified its ground and air operations, wiping out entire families in what is being described as an increasingly restricted urban battlefield where civilians have [no mobility or safe refuge](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/21/israeli-strikes-kill-entire-families-in-gaza).
* The United Nations has issued a "red alert" for the besieged city of El Obeid in Sudan, where paramilitary drone strikes and 18 months of warfare are starving the population and destroying the social fabric, precipitating a [severe humanitarian catastrophe](https://www.dw.com/en/sudan-crisis).

## DEMOCRACY AND VOTING
* In a move that undermines a shared factual reality, the FBI has deployed over 200 analysts to "surge" an investigation into the 2020 election in Fulton County, Georgia, breathing new life into long-debunked fraud claims that [intimidate election workers and threaten democratic institutions](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jul/02/fbi-orders-staffers-georgia-2020-election-investigation).
* The newly proposed SAVE America Act is drawing intense criticism from voting rights advocates in the United States, who warn the legislation will heavily restrict the franchise and deliberately disenfranchise [minorities and military personnel](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/voting-rights-save-act) by making it significantly harder to cast a ballot.
* Ahead of upcoming elections, international monitors are warning that ongoing restrictions on political discourse and free expression in Algeria risk [robbing citizens of their democratic sovereignty](https://www.article19.org/resources/algeria-freedom-of-expression-is-essential-to-democratic-elections/), emphasizing that a free press is essential to prevent autocracy.

## FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION
* Saudi Arabia is arresting religious pilgrims and visitors, sentencing them to lengthy prison terms over simple social media posts—a draconian security measure that completely destroys any capacity for [free digital expression](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2026/06/visitors-to-saudi-arabia-imprisoned-for-social-media-posts/).
* A newly proposed "Cybercrimes Bill 2026" in Malaysia is raising alarms among privacy advocates, who argue the legislation uses national security as a pretext to expand state surveillance, threatening [online autonomy and independent expression](https://www.article19.org/resources/malaysia-cybercrimes-bill-2026-threat-to-free-expression/).
* The UN Secretary-General has condemned the recent detention of political and civic actors in Uganda, demanding the government immediately restore the basic constitutional rights of [liberty and independent media](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/uganda-rights) to allow for genuinely free and unpredictable political choices.

## ECONOMIC POWER
* Facing an extreme concentration of computational power and wealth among artificial intelligence companies, US lawmakers are debating a 50% tax on large AI firms to create a sovereign wealth fund, aiming to build [empowering collective structures](https://apnews.com/article/bernie-sanders-ai-sovereign-wealth-fund-technology) rather than allowing tech oligarchs to automate away jobs and deepen inequality.
* A new economic analysis reveals that the 100 largest European corporations are allocating 70% of their profits to shareholder dividends, systematically enriching a wealthy elite at the expense of workers and [widening the continent's structural inequality](https://www.oxfam.org/en/press-releases/people-power-profits-planet-how-biggest-european-companies-fuel-global-inequality).
* Environmental defenders and progressive groups in Latin America are urging governments to establish binding global rules against "corporate capture," warning that unchecked multinational monopolies continue to [drive aggressive resource extractivism](https://www.escr-net.org/news/2026/no-democracy-without-rules-corporate-power) and systematically disempower local communities.
